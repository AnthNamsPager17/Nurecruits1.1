Single Board Computers

What are single board computers?

Single board computers (SBC’s) are complete computers built on a single circuit board. They operate through the use of:
• A computer processor where the data processing logic and control is included on a single integrated Circuit, or a small number of integrated circuits, known as a microprocessor. • A random-access Memory device (RAM) that allows data to be read and changed in any necessary order. The random-access memory is used to hold working data and machine code necessary for the use of the SBC. • A system for communication between operating systems, the computer and the external world, be it a human or another processing system, referred to as Input/output (io’s or IO’s).

The most common use of a single board computer is in the use of embedded applications. an embedded computer refers to a device whose capabilities are restricted to the requirements of the task for which it has been designed. Unlike other apps and tools, it cannot be further expanded to contain other capabilities, this is what makes SBC’s the most appropriate tool for some jobs as SBC’s work without the need of expansion slots to perform peripheral tasks and expansions. SBC’s applications expand out to home computers, portable devises, typically gaming (slot machines and video poker), kiosks, and machine control automation.

SBC’s are cheap and easily produced their light weight and compact size enables speedy shipping. Compared to laptops and personal computers SBC’s simplicity increases reliability and power efficiency.

Single Board Computers date back to 1976, (The first true single-board computer made an appearance in the May 1976 issue of Radio-Electronics), it was named "dyna-micro". However, they gained a large increase in notability after the invention of the raspberry pi.

What is a Raspberry Pi?

The Raspberry pi is an SBC created for educational purposes, it is a handheld device (often compared to the size of a credit card) with the intended use of tutoring programming languages like scratch and python, whilst still replicating the abilities of a desktop computer. It soon became the most Popular SBC after its release in 2013, in thanks to its low cost, ordered rows general of purpose input/output pins (GPIO’s) that allow it to interact with keyboards, mouse, computer monitors and other devices. The commonality of these devices also makes them very appealing to the inexperienced and experiences alike in the fields of programming and computing The field of SBCs is ever expanding, but the Raspberry pi has proven itself to be the head of the spear when it comes to SBCs, as the most sold and supported tool available. The Raspberry pi is what most would consider the state of the art SBC for 2021, and is used to teach Children computer science in schools internationally as well as other peoples in low-income countries the Raspberry Pi’s ability to interact with the outside world can be used in a wide array of digital maker projects, from music machines, weather stations and basic robotics.
