What does Cybersecurity do?

Cybercrime is a form of warfare used through the cyber world that attack system hardware, software, and networks. The role in cybersecurity is to understand how the IT components of a company are designed with frequent concentration toward detail, and the continuous observation and assessments of any threats that violate a company’s network. The main function of cybersecurity is to maintain and discover various approaches on defending and fortifying crucial data, using various means such as,

  1. Using password defence applications, anti-virus platforms and exposure control software’s.
  2. Reporting on current events within a network and analyse its strong points, with an ability to review the report in detail, that gives an overview of how effective the data defences are and identify any strange activity in the network.
  3. Identifying any weak points in the network, by continuously examining the network before any bad actors or outside threats that try to concede it. Reference for entire paragraph: (W.G.U, n.d.)

What is the state of the art of this new technology?

There are several high-quality bits of technology that play an important role in cyber crime prevention, they not only include physical technology but can also include software updates and policies that can be implemented to improve the privacy and protection of personal information and not impede on any governing laws within a particular country. Some of these technologies include Two-Factor Authentication, behaviour analytics, pin and/or password protection and blockchain cybersecurity (I.F.F, 2019).

What can be done now?

    Although there is a lack of awareness around the world about how serious cybercrime is, there are many things that can be done today to help you to protect your important data from being in the wrong hands, this includes,
  • Designing effective policies and methods for workers and companies
  • Setting up a security system to guard information and networks
  • Confirming purchasing requests prior to operations
  • Designing a system that can identify fake emails
  • Cybersecurity awareness education on policies and methods to all employees
  • Maintaining up to date with latest versions of software on networks, devices, and software
  • Creating a backup of data and information on a constant basis
  • Caution of unexpected email links
  • Avoid downloading from unverified sources (unknown sources)
  • Keeping away from using public Wi-Fi connectivity
  • Setting all social interaction profiles to private
  • Double thinking everything sold online
  • Activating privacy settings on social networks
  • Monitoring of kid’s activities through parental controls, by limiting access to what kids can view online
  • Contacting local authorities if you suffered from any identity theft, child exploitation or commercial scams Reference: (Raj Singh Deora, 2021) Even though these methods can help to improve cybersecurity the main concern surrounding it, is that as more devices connect, the harder it is to close the gap for security measures. In saying this, risk management has been a recent methodology adopted by many organisations around the world, in an attempt to close the gap (Kosub, 2015). Reasons for this is that as technology advances so does cybercrime, which in turn requires more cybersecurity efforts in establishing effective protection.

What is likely to change?

Cyber threats never stay the same, they are always evolving into something bigger and more potent. There are even some security threats right now that do not carry a detectable digital signature ( Hugh Taylor, 16.7.21). The best practice for defending against a cyber security threat is as simple as patching systems. A company will issue updates to their systems due to the fact they have found an exploit and need to fix it. Educating people on why it is important to do these updates is also an excellent step in helping reduce the number of personal hacks that can occur.
